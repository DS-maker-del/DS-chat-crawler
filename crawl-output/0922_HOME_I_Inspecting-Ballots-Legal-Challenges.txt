Election materials belong to the association but remain in the custody of the inspector of elections or at a location designated by the inspector until after the time allowed by&nbsp; Section 5145 &nbsp;for&nbsp;challenging the election&nbsp;has expired ( one year ), at which time custody of the records may be transferred to the association. ( Civ. Code &sect; 5125 ) Starting January 1, 2020, members are allowed to&nbsp;inspect election materials. ( Civ. Code &sect; 5200(c) ) Election materials include:&nbsp;candidate registration list,&nbsp;voter list,&nbsp;ballots,&nbsp;signed voter envelopes (may be inspected but not copied), the voter list of names, parcel numbers, and voters to whom ballots were to be sent, proxies, the candidate registration list, and the tally sheet of votes cast by electronic secret ballot. ( Civ. Code&nbsp;&sect; 5200(c) ) Proxies . Proxies are not included in the authorizing language for inspections; only ballots are included. The reason for not including proxies may be that they are personal and confidential between the member and his/her proxy and do not impact the inspection of ballots. However, if the portion of the proxy with instructions on how to vote is used as the ballot, it can be inspected.&nbsp; Legal Challenges Any director or any person who had the right to vote in the election has standing to challenge the election. ( Civ. Code &sect; 5145(a) ,&nbsp; Corp. Code &sect; 7616(a) )&nbsp;As provided in&nbsp; Civil Code &sect; 5145(c) , owners may bring actions in&nbsp; small claims court &nbsp;for the following under&nbsp; Civil Code &sect; 5105(a)&nbsp;and&nbsp; Civil Code&nbsp;&sect; 5120 .&nbsp;In addition to levying fines, small claims judges may grant&nbsp; equitable and injunctive relie f ( Code Civ. Proc. &sect; 116.220 ) and also levy penalties of up to $500 per violation ( Civ.
