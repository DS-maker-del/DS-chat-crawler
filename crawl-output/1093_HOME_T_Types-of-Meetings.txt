board Voting - membership Waiver of Liability Warranties Washer/Dryer Wasps Water conservation Water damage Websites Wheelchair access Wi-Fi Wild animals Workers compensation Yard sale Zero-base budgeting Zinsco panels Zoning Zoom meetings TYPES OF ASSOCIATION MEETINGS Print &nbsp; Share &nbsp; Report Broken Links Associations have different kinds of meetings with different notice requirements for each and different rights attached to the meetings. They can be divided into three distinct types: Board Meetings Open Meetings . Members can attend to observe the board conduct business (either in-person or virtual meetings ). &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; - Regular (scheduled same day each month; requires 4-days&#39; notice) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; - Special (for matters that cannot wait for a regularly scheduled meeting; requires 4 days&#39; notice) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; - Emergency (to address emergencies, no notice required) Closed ( executive session ) Meetings . Members cannot attend due to the confidential nature of the topics discussed. Executive session meetings can be held before or after an open board meeting or as a stand-alone meeting on a different day from an open meeting, provided two days&#39; notice is given (unless it&#39;s an emergency executive session meeting). They can be in-person or virtual (Zoom) meetings . Committee Meetings 1.&nbsp; &nbsp; Standing Committees (long-term committees) 2.&nbsp; &nbsp;Ad Hoc (special purpose advisory) 3.&nbsp; &nbsp; Executive Committee (directors only) Membership Meetings 1.&nbsp; &nbsp; Annual Meetings . Annual meetings are required by the governing documents for the election of directors. 2.&nbsp; &nbsp; Special Meetings . Special membership meetings are held for a specific purpose to vote on a matter, such as (i)&nbsp; amending&nbsp;governing documents , (ii) approving special assessments, (iii) granting exclusive use to common areas, and (iv)&nbsp; removing directors from the board . 3.&nbsp; &nbsp; Town Hall meetings . Informational meetings where no business is conducted. Joint Neighborhood Meetings Whenever two or more associations have consolidated any of their functions under a joint neighborhood association or similar organization, members of each participating association shall be (a) entitled to attend all meetings of the joint association other than executive sessions, (b) given reasonable opportunity for participation in those meetings, and (c) entitled to the same access to the joint association&rsquo;s records as they are to the participating association&rsquo;s records.&nbsp;( Civ.
