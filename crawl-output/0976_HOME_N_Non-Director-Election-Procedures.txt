Special Membership Meetings Boards of directors may call special membership meetings to vote on special assessments , to amend/restate CC&amp;Rs and Bylaws, and to vote on grants of exclusive use of the common area . Code&nbsp;&sect; 5105(i)(1) )&nbsp;the notice must be sent by individual&nbsp;notice and must include (i) the member&rsquo;s&nbsp;current voting method; (ii)&nbsp;the email address that will be used; (iii)&nbsp;an explanation of how to opt out of electronic voting; and (iv)&nbsp;the deadline for opting out. Prepare Ballots&nbsp; Ballots must state the applicable quorum(s) and approval requirements for matters to be voted&nbsp;on and the date and time by which the ballot must be received, and if the date may be extended to achieve a quorum or allow&nbsp;additional&nbsp;participation by the members. Distribute Ballot Materials and Election Rules At least&nbsp; 30 days&nbsp; before the deadline for voting and at least&nbsp; 30 days &nbsp;after the pre-ballot notice was sent, the&nbsp; inspector of elections &nbsp;or the&nbsp;association&nbsp;must mail (first-class) or otherwise deliver&nbsp; ballots and&nbsp;pre-addressed envelopes &nbsp;with instructions on how to return them. ( Civ. Code &sect; 5115(c) .) Election Rules &nbsp;must also be delivered to members. ( Civ. Code &sect; 5105(h)(4) .) Delivery may be accomplished by either of the following methods: &nbsp; &bull; Posting the election operating rules to an internet website and including the corresponding internet website address on the ballot together with the phrase, in at least 12-point type: &ldquo;The rules governing this election may be found here:&rdquo;&nbsp; &nbsp; &bull; Individual delivery (mailed).&nbsp; Deliver individual notice of the electronic ballot to each member&nbsp; 30 days &nbsp;before the election, along with instructions on (i) how to obtain access to that internet-based voting system and (ii) how to vote by electronic secret ballot. ( Civ. Code&nbsp;&sect; 5105(i)(3) .) Paper ballots must be delivered to members who choose to vote by paper and to those members who do not provide a working email address to the association.&nbsp;( Civ. Code&nbsp;&sect; 5105(i)(1)(C) .) Code&nbsp;&sect; 5105(i)(7) .) For governing document amendments : The text of the proposed amendment may be sent electronically with the ballots. The association must also deliver a writen copy of the text of the proposed amendment to members upon request and without charge.&nbsp; Election Rules &nbsp;must also be delivered to members.&nbsp; Civ. Delivery may be accomplished by either of the following methods: &nbsp; &bull; Posting the election operating rules to an internet website and including the corresponding internet website address on the ballot together with the phrase, in at least 12-point type: &nbsp;&ldquo;The rules governing this election may be found here:&rdquo;&nbsp; &nbsp; &bull; Individual delivery (mailed).&nbsp; Votes Are Irrevocable All votes must be counted and tabulated by the inspector&nbsp;of elections (or designees of the inspector) in public at a properly noticed open meeting of the board or members. Any candidate or other member of the association may witness the tabulation of votes. Ballots may not be opened before the time and place at which they are publicly tabulated. The inspector or designees may verify the member&rsquo;s information and signature on the outer envelope prior to the meeting at which ballots are tabulated. Once a ballot is received by the inspector, it shall be irrevocable. ( Civ. Code &sect; 5120(a) .) A&nbsp;vote cast electronically is irrevocable. ( Civ. Code&nbsp;&sect; 5105(i)(6) .) No person shall open or otherwise review any tally sheet of votes cast by electronic secret ballots before the time and place at which the ballots are counted and tabulated. ( Civ. Code &sect; 5120(c) .) Ballot Counting Meeting The meeting is&nbsp;called to order. &bull; Call for any additional balloting&nbsp;if the voting period is still&nbsp;open. Close polls. &bull; Inspector(s) and designated assistants may open ballots and tally votes. &bull; Results announced, recorded in the next board meeting minutes and provided by general notice (e.g., posted) within 15 days . The meeting is called to order. &bull; Maintain a tally sheet of votes cast by electronic secret ballot. &bull; Results announced, recorded in the next board meeting minutes and provided by general notice (e.g., posted) within 15 days . Storing Election Materials Election materials remain in the custody of the&nbsp; inspector of elections &nbsp;or at a location designated by the inspector until after the time allowed by&nbsp; Section 5145 &nbsp;for&nbsp; challenging the election &nbsp;has expired ( one year ), at which time custody of the records may be transferred to the association. ( Civ. Code &sect; 5125 .) Starting January 1, 2020, members are allowed to&nbsp; inspect election materials . ( Civ. Code &sect; 5200(c) .)
